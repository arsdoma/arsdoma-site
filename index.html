ARSDOMA

Relational System Design for Human Development

ARSDOMA is a conceptual framework for designing educational and developmental environments that prioritise relational safety, authentic development, and sustainable mental wellbeing.

Rather than focusing solely on performance outcomes, ARSDOMA examines how relational structures shape learning, motivation, and long-term psychological resilience.

Concept

The framework builds on insights from developmental psychology, attachment theory, and systems thinking. It proposes that the quality of the relational field surrounding learners is a primary determinant of cognitive openness, creativity, and meaningful engagement.

Research Direction

Current work explores how educational systems can be designed as “good-enough systems” where relational safety, autonomy, and belonging are structurally supported.
